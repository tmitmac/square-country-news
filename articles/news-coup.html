Political Instability as Rumours Swirl of a Potential Leadership Coup

The Prime Minister could be facing a possible leadership challenge by former Prime Minister Trent McKenzie.
James Holloway Senior Political Correspondent
April 18, 2026 · 6 min read

Coup Rumours Swirl Around Prime Minister Parker Ahead of Tense Caucus Meeting

Speculation over the stability of Prime Minister Ryan Parker’s leadership has intensified following claims that former Prime Minister Trent McKenzie has been holding private discussions with several Members of Parliament, including Liberty Party backbenchers, at his residence earlier this week.

Sources allege the gathering involved a number of MPs entering McKenzie’s home over a short period of time, prompting rumours that internal discussions may have centred on a possible leadership spill or broader challenge to Parker’s authority. While none of the individuals involved have confirmed the nature of the meeting, the timing has fuelled growing political unease.

This comes shortly following an apparent demotion for McKenzie, after losing the finance portfolio to the Prime Minister himself.

Tuesday's Caucus Meeting Will Tell All

Attention is now firmly fixed on Tuesday’s scheduled caucus meeting, which is expected to be a key moment in determining whether any internal divisions within the governing bloc become publicly exposed. Several MPs are understood to be under pressure to clarify their positions ahead of the meeting, amid rising speculation of factional tension.

Prime Minister Ryan Parker has dismissed the reports as “a media attempt to scrutinize the Liberty Part,” stating that the government remains stable and focused on its current legislative programme ahead of the general election in October. “My Government is working hard for the people of Square Country,” the Prime Minister said.

McKenzie has so far declined to comment, while opposition figures have also remained largely silent. However, political observers note that informal meetings between senior and former political figures often attract scrutiny, particularly when they coincide with heightened internal debate.

Looking Ahead

With Parliament due to return next week, all eyes will be on Tuesday’s caucus meeting to see whether the rumours fade—or develop into something more significant.
