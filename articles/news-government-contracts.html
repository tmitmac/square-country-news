Opposition Calls for Investigation Into Government Contracts

Freedoms, Greens, Innovation, and Square Country First demand transparency over millions in public funds awarded to private firms.
Hannah Whitaker Political Reporter
February 17, 2026 · 6 min read

Opposition parties have jointly called for a formal investigation into a series of government contracts awarded by the Liberty administration, raising concerns about transparency and the use of taxpayer funds.

The Freedoms Party, Greens, Innovation Party, and Square Country First have questioned the process behind several major infrastructure and consulting contracts worth hundreds of millions of dollars.

Freedoms leader Marcus Hale said the public deserved clear answers. “These are taxpayer funds, and taxpayers deserve full transparency,” he said. “We need to ensure proper process was followed and that there was no political favouritism.”

Greens Demand Independent Oversight

The Greens echoed the call, with co-leader Amelia Varga urging the creation of an independent inquiry. “Public trust depends on accountability,” she said. “An independent investigation is essential to ensure integrity.”

Innovation Party finance spokesperson Daniel Cho said Parliament’s oversight mechanisms must be used fully. “This is not about politics,” he said. “It’s about protecting public confidence in government institutions.”

Government Rejects Allegations

The Liberty Government has strongly rejected the opposition’s claims, insisting all contracts were awarded according to proper procedures.

Finance Minister Ryan Parker said the accusations were politically motivated. “Every contract was awarded following strict procurement rules,” he said. “This is nothing more than an attempt by a divided opposition to create controversy.”

Election Issue Emerging

The dispute comes as political tensions rise ahead of the October general election, with opposition parties increasingly scrutinising the Government’s record.

Analysts say the issue could become a major campaign battleground, particularly if further details emerge in the coming months.

Despite their shared criticism of the Government, divisions remain between opposition parties on other key issues, raising questions about their ability to work together if Liberty loses its majority.

For now, the Government has refused to launch a formal inquiry, but pressure is expected to intensify when Parliament resumes next week.
