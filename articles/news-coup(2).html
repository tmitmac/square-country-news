Prime Minister Survives Leadership Spill, Arthur Bexley set to resign

Ryan Parker has survived a sudden leadership challenge during the Libery Party caucus meeting this morning.
April Sanderson Political Correspondent
May 21, 2026 · 6 min read

The Prime Minister has survived a sudden leadership motion by his Minister of Foreign Affairs, Arthur Bexley, dealing the biggest blow to government stability since rumours emerged of a potential coup, led by former Prime Minister Trent McKenzie, in April.

During the Liberty Party caucus meeting this morning, the Prime Minister was challenged by Arthur Bexley, a frontbench Minister who has grown increasingly dissatisfied with Parker's leadership.

11-4

Ryan Parker survived the motion by 11 votes to 4, a safe result. However, it draws attention to the other three votes against the Prime Minister. Last month, former Prime Minister Trent McKenzie had been seen hosting a meeting with several other Liberty Party MPs, one of which was Arthur Bexley. At this stage we do not know in which ways Liberty MPs voted, but the question must be asked: is McKenzie plotting to overthrow Parker, like Parker did to him late last year?

Prime Minister Comments

When asked about growing discontent in his own caucus, the Prime Minister flat-out denied mass unease. "The actions taking by the Member in the caucus room here today was out of spite and is not a representation of the caucus as a whole. My government is focused on delivering for Square Country and I will not stop now because of this road bump.

It comes at a difficult time for the government, as the 2026 Budget is expected to be released next week, and has already faced challenges from former Liberty Finance Minister Ruth Lockwood and treasury officals. Instead, much of the budget will be overshadowed by today's events. "I have big plans for Budget 2026 and will not stop now as the people of this country depend on this government to not stop," Parker said.

Opposition Responds

Leader of the Opposition David Campbell has weighed in on the unfolding events in the Liberty Party caucus. "What we're seeing here is a government that can't even hold itself together," Campbell said. "The Prime Minister said that we are not ready to lead, but looking at what's happened today, Ryan Parker can't even lead his own party."

Latest polls show a significant bump in popularity for the Freedoms Party, who have been in opposition since 2014. Prospects of a coalition between opposition parties is becoming more possible as the election draws closer.

End of the Road for Arthur Bexley

Arthur Bexley has already alluded to his impending resignation from all ministerial portfolios, including foreign affairs. This coming just weeks before the North Pacific Leaders Meeting in Washington DC, where Bexley was set to attend.

"I simply cannot continue to work under such terrible leadership," Bexley said. “Ryan Parker is incompetent and incapable of providing a steady and stable government for Square Country. It is clear that my caucus colleagues do not agree with me, and so it is unlikely that I will remain in Parliament past this week."

Impacts on Election 2026

Historically, the electorate does not like instability in government. Leadership spills are a rare thing to occur in Square Country, but it is more than likely that this will impact Liberty's results as they try to secure a fifth term in power.
