Budget 2026 Targets Growth, Jobs, and Infrastructure, Promising Significant Investment

The Finance Minister has revealed the highly-anticipated 2026 Budget, promising significant investment in all sectors.
John Appleford Political Economic Correspondent
June 9, 2026 · 10 min read

Finance Minister Ryan Parker has revealed the contents of the greatly anticipated budget he has named the "Think Big Budget".

Historic Changes to Income Tax

"Think Big" has revealed immense changes to the current income tax regime. From 1 July, the first $25,000 of income will be taxed at zero percent. Ryan Parker says that this will protect low and middle income earners. Treasury forcasts that more than 10 million Squares will pay $0 in tax on that portion of their earnings. "Our tax system should reward hard work, not punish it,' Finance Minister Ryan Parker said.

Changes to GST

In a bid to improve healthcare for Squares, the government has announced the removal of Goods and Service Tax (GST) on all medicine. Various community groups and healthcare adovcates have praised the change, citing a failing healthcare system around the country. The Ministry of Finance has confirmed that GST will remain 15% on all other goods and services.

Northern Prosperity Plan

Earlier this year, Prime Minister Ryan Parker announced the Northern Propseprtiy Plan, as the Liberty Party describes it a "$1.8–2.5 billion five-year commitment to unlock the full economic potential of Square Country's North." This plan has been further reiterated in the "Think Big Budget", which is offering new Northern Investment Zones, a region-wide infrastructure push, and local industry and export support. Although, the plan has drawn criticism from Leader of the Opposition David Campbell, who argues that the government should be investing in all of Square Country, not just a select few regions."

Books in Surplus

Perhaps the biggest news, is Square Country has finally returned to surplus. The Finance Minister announced that the books are in the black by more than $19 billion, following years of norrow defecit plaguing the government. Ryan Parker says "the surplus is a result of significant work done by the Ministry [of Finance] and the government."

Opposition Largely in Favour of Budget

In an unlikely twist, the majority of the opposition bloc has stood behind the "Think Big Budget", offering their support for changes to tax and investment in healthcare, education, and infrastructure. However, there have been several smaller issues between the various opposition parties. The Square Country Greens were the only party to vote against the budget, citing a lack of investment in public transport, and a signficant level of spending. "The Finance Minister simply cannot put together a budget to save his life. From our perspective, there is far too little investment in public transport. If Square Country could borrow more, we could achieve the things that we need to achieve," Erin Starling said. Ryan Parker wrote off Starlings claims as "pointless. This is what we see from the Greens time and again: tax more, borrow more, spend more. It's a wonder they've even mad eit this far with that ideology."

Voter Priorities

The latest Square Country News poll showed that the economy was at the forefront of voters' minds, with the 2026 Budget having the potential to make or break Liberty's chances at reelection in October.

130 Days

The October General Election is just 130 daysaway. It is clear that the Liberty Party had a lot invested in this budget. Economists around the country are in support of the fiscal management shown in "Think Big" and it is more than likely that will translate to support in the electorate. For now, the Minister of Finance has a major task on his hands: implementing such ambitious goals set in this years budget.
