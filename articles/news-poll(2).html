Latest Poll Shows Shifts in Party Support Ahead of October Election

Liberty holds a narrow 1-seat majority, while minor parties—including Greens, Innovation, and Square Country First—show significant gains, indicating a more fragmented Parliament ahead.
James Holloway Political Correspondent
February 17, 2026 · 10 min read

The latest nationwide poll indicates a shifting political landscape in Square Country, with the governing Liberty Party holding only a 1-seat majority in Parliament while minor parties are on the rise. The survey, conducted over the past fortnight, signals a potentially volatile election in October.

Poll Results: Party Vote and Seat Allocation

The poll shows the following projected results if the election were held today:

Party Party Vote (%) Projected Seats (26 total)
Liberty Party 37% 14
Freedoms Party 21% 6
Greens 14% 3
Innovation Party 11% 2
Square Country First 7% 1

Minor Parties Gaining Ground

The Greens, Innovation Party, and Square Country First are showing noticeable growth in both vote share and seat projections. Analysts say these gains reflect voter frustration with the two major parties, Liberty and Freedoms, and indicate that Parliament could be more fragmented after the election.

Liberty’s Slim Majority

With 14 out of 26 seats, Liberty maintains only a 1-seat majority. Party strategists are focusing on key constituencies to protect the Government’s slim lead. Political experts warn that even minor swings in voter sentiment could threaten Liberty’s dominance.

Freedoms Decline

Freedoms’ projected 6 seats represent a decline, illustrating the party’s struggle to maintain relevance. Internal divisions and competition from rising minor parties have contributed to its diminishing support.

Potential Coalition Implications

The growing influence of minor parties means a slim majority may be difficult to sustain, and coalition discussions could become essential if Liberty loses seats on election day. Tensions between Freedoms and Greens suggest that forming an opposition coalition would not be straightforward.

Voter Priorities

The poll also shows climate policy, economic security, and government accountability as top issues for voters. Minor parties are increasingly seen as more responsive on these matters, helping explain their rise in support.

Looking Ahead

The October election could see a dramatically reshaped Parliament, with minor parties holding the balance of power. Political commentators suggest every seat will matter, and Liberty’s narrow majority may be tested if current trends continue.
